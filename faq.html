Frequently Asked Questions

A Bushlot sauna is built on a 5×10 utility trailer with a standard 2-inch ball hitch. It tows like any other trailer — no special equipment or commercial license required. A standard full-size pickup truck or SUV with a tow hitch can handle it.

You can position it in your backyard, park it lakeside, haul it to a campsite, or store it in a garage between seasons. The only requirement is a road-accessible site with enough clearance to maneuver a trailer.

A standard full-size pickup truck or body-on-frame SUV with a 2-inch ball hitch is sufficient. We'll provide you with the trailer's loaded weight prior to delivery so you can confirm your vehicle's tow capacity. Most half-ton trucks and larger SUVs are well within range.

If you're unsure whether your vehicle qualifies, ask us when you inquire and we'll help you work it out.

Four people comfortably. The interior bench layout is designed for a full sauna experience at capacity — not a tight squeeze. It's sized well for family use, friend groups, or couples who want room to breathe.

It starts with a conversation — use the contact form to reach out and tell us what you're thinking. We'll respond to discuss your customization options, answer any questions, and give you a complete picture of the build and cost.

Once you decide to move forward, we'll agree on your selections (stove type, interior wood, exterior finish), establish a build timeline, and discuss deposit and payment terms. We typically require a deposit at time of order to cover materials, with the balance due upon delivery.

Build time is several weeks depending on the current schedule. We'll keep you informed throughout. At delivery, we'll walk you through everything in person.

A typical build takes several weeks from materials ready to completion. The exact timeline depends on your customization choices, current build schedule, and material lead times. We'll give you a specific estimate when you inquire.

Our primary trade area is Western North Carolina, with emphasis on the greater Asheville metro (Buncombe, Henderson, Haywood, and Madison counties).
